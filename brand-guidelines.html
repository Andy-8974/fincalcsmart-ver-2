01 — Brand Overview
Who We Are
Clear. Trustworthy. Free.
FinCalc Smart is a free financial calculator platform built for Canadians and Americans who need real answers — without sign-ups, paywalls, or jargon.
🎯
Purpose
Empower people to make informed financial decisions with free, accurate, instantly-useful tools.
👥
Audience
North Americans across all demographics — first-home buyers, FIRE seekers, students, retirees, professionals.
⚡
Promise
No login. No paywall. No ads in calculators. Just instant, correct answers with charts and context.
02 — Mission & Values
Brand Personality
What FinCalc Smart stands for
These four attributes define every design decision, piece of content, and product choice we make.
Trustworthy
We show our work. Formulas, assumptions, and disclaimers are always visible. We never exaggerate results or hide limitations.
Educational
Every calculator teaches. Labels, callouts, and guides explain not just what the number is, but what it means for your financial life.
Accessible
No finance degree required. We avoid jargon. When technical terms are necessary, we explain them immediately and plainly.
Neutral
FinCalc Smart is not a bank, broker, or advisor. We have no financial interest in your decisions. Our only goal is accuracy.

11 — Callouts & Alerts
Components
Callout Boxes
Three callout variants communicate different message types. Always include a bold title and short explanatory text.
✅ Teal — Tips, Canada-specific rules, positive outcomes

For homes priced $500,000–$999,999, the minimum down payment is 5% on the first $500k and 10% on the remainder. A 20% down payment eliminates CMHC insurance entirely.

⚠️ Amber — Warnings, caveats, things to watch out for

Always confirm your lender's prepayment allowance. Some discount mortgages restrict or eliminate prepayment options — that low rate may cost you more if your situation changes.

ℹ️ Navy/Gray — Neutral context, definitions, key distinctions

CMHC insurance protects your lender, not you. If you default, CMHC pays the lender — then pursues you for recovery. This is different from homeowner's insurance.

13 — Voice & Tone
Content
Voice & Tone Guidelines
FinCalc Smart communicates with authority but without arrogance. Educational and neutral — like a knowledgeable friend who happens to be a financial expert.
Our voice is:
✓
Clear — Plain language, no unnecessary jargon
✓
Educational — We explain, not just calculate
✓
Data-driven — Real numbers, cited sources
✓
Neutral — No product recommendations or bias
✓
Canadian-first — TFSA before Roth IRA, CMHC before FHA
Our voice is NOT:
✗
Salesy — No "amazing deal" or urgency pressure
✗
Alarmist — We inform, not scare
✗
Preachy — No moral judgements on financial decisions
✗
Vague — Always use real numbers and specifics
✗
Overconfident — Always include "consult a professional"
✓ Do — Clear, specific, educational

"A single percentage point difference costs over $100,000 in total interest on a typical Canadian mortgage. This is why shopping rates — even for 0.25% — is worth the effort."

✗ Don't — Vague, salesy, no data

"Mortgage rates can vary a lot! Make sure you get the best deal by comparing different lenders. You could save a ton of money!"

✓ Do — Neutral disclaimer

"This article is for educational purposes only and does not constitute financial or mortgage advice. Consult a licensed mortgage broker or financial advisor before making borrowing decisions."

✗ Don't — Missing disclaimer

"Based on these numbers, you should definitely choose the 25-year amortization. It's clearly the better option for your situation."

15 — Do's & Don'ts
Usage
Brand Usage Rules
These rules ensure FinCalc Smart looks consistent and professional across all touchpoints — website, social, email, and third-party integrations.
✓ Design Do's
  • Use the sandwich layout: dark navy hero → white body → dark navy footer
  • Show the country toggle (USA/Canada) on any calculator with regional differences
  • Always include a disclaimer on calculator results and blog articles
  • Use Plus Jakarta Sans at 800–900 weight for all headlines
  • Apply the amber color (#C9A84C) exclusively to calculator icons
  • Use the 2-column input grid to reduce vertical scrolling in calculators
  • Maintain 16px minimum clearspace around the FinCalc Smart wordmark
✗ Design Don'ts
  • Don't use solid dark backgrounds for the main content body area
  • Don't use emoji flags — always use SVG flag icons
  • Don't place the "On This Page" / Table of Contents in blog sidebars
  • Don't use purple gradients, Inter font, or generic AI design patterns
  • Don't use initFaqs() event listeners — use onclick="toggleFaq(this)"
  • Don't nest blog-grid inside sec-top flex containers
  • Don't use hardcoded paths for blog pages — use isBlogPage() in shared.js
✓ Content Do's
  • Lead with Canada-specific content, follow with USA comparison
  • Always include real dollar amounts in examples (not just percentages)
  • End every blog article with a relevant calculator CTA
  • Include 3 FAQ questions per article with working accordion
  • Credit Unsplash for photography and include descriptive alt text
  • Update all year references (2026) in disclaimers and rate tables annually
✗ Content Don'ts
  • Don't provide specific financial advice or product recommendations
  • Don't publish without a disclaimer on every calculator and article page
  • Don't use 2025 date references — all content is updated to 2026
  • Don't link to competitor calculators or financial product sign-up pages
  • Don't quote specific current rates without noting they change frequently
